A place for sketches, systems, and experiments that turn code into image, rhythm, and texture.

I wanted this page to feel closer to the work itself: small visual ideas, generative studies, AI-assisted trials, and the kinds of code explorations that start with curiosity more than with a product goal.

Why this exists

Some projects begin as a line of code, a strange output, or a prompt worth pushing further. This page gathers that side of the practice.

Why this exists

Closer to the studio side of code

What is of interests to me is not just code as performance or code as product, but code as material: something you can sketch with, repeat, distort, train, and translate into visuals. Sometimes that means generative patterns. Sometimes it means using AI as a partner for variation, selection, or surprise. The point is to explore what new forms appear when technical tools are used with an artistic intention.

Make things

Some repositories begin with a visual hunch and turn into motion, texture, or interaction.

Learn by seeing

Seeing an output change in real time is often the fastest way to understand what a system can do.

Bridge disciplines

Art, generative logic, and AI become most interesting when they are treated as parts of the same studio practice.

Repositories to explore

Ways into the work

These directions are less about categories and more about entry points: places to explore motion, pattern, interfaces, prompts, and visual systems through code. They show different ways art can be built, not just displayed.

These are references I would actually want near this page: artists and creative coders whose repositories show what happens when math, systems, shaders, loops, plotters, or prompts become part of an artistic language.

Anders Hoff inconvergent

Organic algorithmic art in Common Lisp

A strong reference for code that feels alive, irregular, and deeply system-driven rather than purely decorative.

github.com/inconvergent
Matt DesLauriers mattdesl

Workshops and generative art studies

One of the clearest entry points into creative coding with JavaScript, sketches, process, and visual experimentation.

github.com/mattdesl/workshop-generative-art
Manolo Gamboa Naon manoloide

Math, algorithms, and pen plotter experiments

Useful for seeing how structure, geometry, and controlled randomness can become a visual signature.

github.com/manoloide
Dave Whyte beesandbombs

Looping animations and Processing studies

A reference for timing, repetition, and visual precision in algorithmic animation.

github.com/beesandbombs
Eric Davids erdavids

Structure, chance, and generative composition

A good example of work that balances control and surprise without losing coherence.

github.com/erdavids/Generative-Art
Eric Yd ericyd

Generative art with Kotlin, Rust, Processing, and shaders

Useful when the page wants to show that creative coding is not tied to one language or one visual tradition.

github.com/ericyd/generative-art
Tyler Hobbs tylerxhobbs

Generative visual algorithms

Fascinating code that bridges the gap between structured programming and organic, natural-looking artwork.

github.com/tylerxhobbs
Gene Kogan genekogan

Machine Learning for Artists

Valuable resources showing how to train models, generate imagery, and use AI practically in creative coding.

github.com/genekogan/ml4a
Mario Klingemann quasimondo

AI art and neural networks experiments

Pioneering work in AI art, demonstrating the unpredictable and surreal outputs of machine learning systems.

github.com/quasimondo
Memo Akten memo

Fluid simulation and AI interaction

Incredible repositories detailing the intersection of human movement, interactive fluids, and machine intelligence.

github.com/memo
Zach Lieberman zachlieberman

Poetic computation and geometry

Daily sketches exploring type, geometry, and motion, deeply influencing modern creative coding.

github.com/zachlieberman
Kyle McDonald kcimc

Open source arts and AI tools

Groundbreaking tools linking optical flow, computer vision, and machine learning into accessible art workflows.

github.com/kcimc
Piter Pasma piterpasma

SDF rendering and generative forms

Highly optimized and deeply abstract generative code, especially known for Signed Distance Fields.

github.com/piterpasma
Daniel Shiffman shiffman

The Nature of Code

The most extensive library of introductory creative coding algorithms simulating physics, biology, and math.

github.com/shiffman
RunwayML runwayml

Machine learning models for creators

Open access to scripts and resources that help artists build, train, and utilize complex AI models.

github.com/runwayml
Frederik Vanhoutte wblut

HE_Mesh and computational geometry

Complex libraries for manipulating meshes and exploring deep 3D computational geometry in Processing.

github.com/wblut
Sougwen Chung sougwen

Robotics, art, and human-machine collaboration

Exploring mark-making alongside robotic arms and capturing the intimate interaction between humans and AI loops.

github.com/sougwen
Jason Ting jzting

Fluid motion and particles

Extensive work with particle simulations and beautiful abstract rendering experiments using open standards.

github.com/jzting
